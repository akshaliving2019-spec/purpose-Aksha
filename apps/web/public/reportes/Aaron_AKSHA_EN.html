Two techniques to ground what you already have

Brian Tracy · Eat the Frog · Ground

The task you avoid most is the first one you do — non-negotiable

Your visionary brain generates 10 ideas before breakfast. That's an advantage — except when none of them get executed because there's always something more interesting on the horizon.

  • The night before: write ONE task. The most important. The one you've postponed the longest.
  • When you wake up: that task is first. Before your phone, before coffee, before everything.
  • Nothing else until it's done. 21 days straight changes the neurological pattern.

Cal Newport · Deep Work · Ground + Air

90 minutes a day without interruptions — that's what separates the person with ideas from the one who executes them

The attention economy in 2026 rewards whoever can hold focus for more than 12 consecutive minutes. That's already a rare and paid skill.

  • Set 1 block of 90 minutes a day: phone on airplane mode, one single task.
  • The first 3 days will feel uncomfortable. That's normal — your brain is detoxing from easy dopamine.
  • In 2 weeks you have more real output than the entire previous month. Measure it.
